<p><p>The man-made regenerated cellulose fibers market is primarily categorized into viscose and lyocell types. Viscose fibers are derived from natural cellulose sources, offering versatility in textiles and a silky texture. They are widely used in clothing and home textiles. In contrast, lyocell fibers are produced through a more eco-friendly process, using a closed-loop system that reduces waste and solvents. Lyocell is known for its strength, moisture-wicking properties, and biodegradability, making it increasingly popular in sustainable fashion.</p></p>
