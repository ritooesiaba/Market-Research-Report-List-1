<p><p>The flame retardant fiber market can be categorized into staple fiber and long fiber types. Staple fibers are shorter lengths and are typically used in products like non-woven fabrics, insulation, and textiles, providing fire resistance through their blended compositions. Long fibers, on the other hand, are continuous lengths used in applications such as woven and knitted fabrics. They offer enhanced strength and durability while maintaining flame retardancy, making them ideal for industrial, automotive, and protective clothing applications.</p></p>
