<p><p>Industrial monocular microscopes are primarily categorized into two types: inverted and upright. Inverted microscopes are designed for observing samples from beneath, making them ideal for live cell imaging and materials testing. They allow for larger specimens and offer greater accessibility to the stage. Upright microscopes, on the other hand, are suited for traditional sample examination from above. They are widely used in quality control and research settings, providing clear visibility for various applications in the industrial sector.</p></p>
