<p><p>Petroleum coke, or petcoke, is a carbon-rich solid derived from petroleum refining. The market is segmented into different types based on their physical characteristics and applications. Needle coke is valued for its conductivity and is primarily used in electrodes for steel and aluminum production. Shot coke is a low-cost, coarse material used in fuels and cement. Sponge coke is porous and often utilized in anodes and fuel. Honeycomb coke, with its unique structure, is also used in specialized applications, including carbon products.</p></p>
