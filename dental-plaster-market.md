<p><p>The dental plaster market is categorized into five types based on their strength and application. Type I is used for making impressions, while Type II is suitable for cast models and can withstand moderate pressure. Type III is stronger, used for producing detailed casts. Type IV offers higher strength and is used in producing working models, ideal for crowns and bridges. Type V is the strongest, designed for high-performance applications, particularly in dental prosthetics where durability is essential.</p></p>
